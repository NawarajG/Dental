Appointment FAQs

Answers to common questions about booking

Please bring your dental insurance card (if applicable), a photo ID, a list of any medications you're currently taking, and any recent dental X-rays (if available). If you're transferring from another dental office, you may want to request that your records be sent to us in advance.

For routine check-ups and cleanings, we recommend scheduling 4-6 weeks in advance. For more urgent needs or specific procedures, please call our office as we may be able to accommodate you sooner. We also keep a cancellation list for patients who wish to be notified of earlier openings.

We require at least 24 hours notice for appointment cancellations or rescheduling. This allows us to offer the time to another patient in need of care. Missed appointments or late cancellations may be subject to a fee, except in cases of emergency.

While we primarily operate by appointment, we do our best to accommodate walk-ins for emergency situations. For non-emergency walk-ins, availability depends on our current schedule. We recommend calling ahead to check availability before coming in.

A standard cleaning and check-up typically takes about 45-60 minutes. More complex procedures or initial visits may take longer (up to 90 minutes). When you schedule your appointment, our staff will provide an estimated time based on the services you're receiving.
